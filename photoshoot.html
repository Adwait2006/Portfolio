Photoshoot -
Gestures of Freedon
[Bharatnatyam]

The photoshoot depicts the transition of a person’s freedom from societal expectations.

It is a progression of 10 photos, of hands in different ‘mudras’ of Bharatnatyam.

The hands are also bound by layers of fabric. Over the course of the photos, the bondage gradually loosens and gives way to a free hand, depicting release from societal expectations.

The mudras of the hands themselves also tell a story. They depict self-expression, as the self expresses itself through the gestures, until its eventual freedom.
